## Abstract

Epileptic seizures are characterized by sudden disruptions in brain activity that can significantly affect a patient's health and quality of life. Accurate and early detection of seizure phases is essential for timely medical intervention. This paper proposes a deep learning-based method to classify EEG signals into three critical phases: interictal, preictal, and ictal. The EEG data is preprocessed using Z-score normalization to ensure consistency across recordings. Spatial features are extracted through Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), while Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) layers are employed to capture temporal dependencies within the signal. The combined CNNLSTM architecture was evaluated on a publicly available labeled EEG dataset. Results demonstrate high classification accuracy, especially in detecting the preictal phase, which is crucial for seizure prediction and prevention. The proposed approach shows strong potential for real-time seizure monitoring and contributes to the advancement of personalized treatment strategies in neurological care.
