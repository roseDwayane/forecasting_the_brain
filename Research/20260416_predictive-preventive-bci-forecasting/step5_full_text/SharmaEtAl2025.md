## Abstract

The objective of this work is real-time seizure prediction and detection in patients with epilepsy utilizing deep neural networks on EEG (electroencephalogram) signals. Epileptic seizures are unstable and have considerable health implications, so early diagnosis is essential to enable timely action. For that purpose, the hybrid deep learning method that involved Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks was examined. Four state-of-the-art CNN-LSTM-based models ChronoNet, CNN-LSTM, EEGNet + LSTM, and ConvLSTM were implemented to extract both spatial and temporal patterns in EEG signals. A total of 3,200 EEG recordings were gathered from several participants over six months, with a series of experiments conducted in controlled clinical settings. The EEG signals were preprocessed with denoising, normalization, segmentation, and feature extraction to make them ready for model training. Each of the models was trained and tested to identify EEG patterns into interictal (normal), preictal (prior to seizure), and ictal (during) states. All of these performances were measured through accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, AUC-ROC, and confusion matrices. Amongst all the models, ChronoNet performed the best with accuracy as 98.76% and AUC-ROC as 0.997, making it the most suitable model to deploy in real-time. This study illustrates the capability of deep learning to provide accurate, efficient, and automatic seizure prediction in wearable and clinical monitoring systems.
